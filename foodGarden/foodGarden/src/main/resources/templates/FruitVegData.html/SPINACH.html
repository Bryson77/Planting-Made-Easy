SPINACH

SPINACH

Growth Duration: Spinach can be planted from August to April. It matures quickly, often within 30–45 days, but continuous harvesting is possible.

Optimal Growing Seasons: Germinates between 2°C and 30°C; optimal growth occurs between 15°C and 18°C. Avoid planting in hot weather.

Water Requirements: Requires constant moisture. Soil should never dry out, especially during early growth. Overhead irrigation or sprinklers are commonly used.

Soil Requirements: Prefers fertile, sandy loam with high organic matter. pH should be between 6.2 and 6.9. Avoid waterlogged or acidic soils.

Sunlight Needs: Tolerates full sun to light shade. In hot climates, partial shade helps prevent bolting.

Fertilizer Needs: Apply phosphorus before planting (56–112 kg/ha). Nitrogen may be added during growth to support leaf production.

Irrigation Needs: Sprinkler irrigation ensures uniform moisture and supports seed germination.

Seed Spacing & Density: Rows should be 50–60 cm apart; plants 15–20 cm apart. Direct seeding depth is about 20 mm.
