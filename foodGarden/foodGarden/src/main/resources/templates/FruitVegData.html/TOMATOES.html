TOMATOES

Growth Duration:

Tomatoes take 60 to over 100 days to harvest depending on the variety. Early types mature faster, while indeterminate varieties produce fruit over a longer period.



Optimal Growing Seasons:

In South Africa, sow seeds between August and January. Start seedlings indoors and transplant after the last frost when soil warms up.



Water Requirements:

Tomatoes need 1–2 inches of water per week. Deep, consistent watering is crucial during flowering and fruiting. Drip irrigation is ideal to reduce disease and conserve water.



Soil Requirements:

Tomatoes prefer well-drained, fertile soil with a slightly acidic pH of 6.2 to 6.8. Adding compost improves structure and nutrient retention.



Sunlight Needs:

Full sun exposure—at least 6 to 8 hours daily—is essential for fruit development.



Temperature Range:

Optimal growth occurs between 26°C and 29°C. Tomatoes tolerate 10°C to 34°C but may suffer in extremes.



Fertilizer Needs:

Use a balanced NPK fertilizer with calcium and magnesium. Avoid excess nitrogen to prevent leafy growth at the expense of fruit.



Irrigation Needs:

Drip irrigation delivers water directly to the root zone, minimizing evaporation and promoting healthy growth.



Seed Spacing & Density:

Space plants 24–36 inches apart; rows 4–5 feet apart to ensure airflow and light penetration.
