ONION

Growth Duration: Onions take 90–100 days from seed, or up to 6 months from planting to harvest. Bulb formation depends on day length and temperature.

Optimal Growing Seasons: Plant from February to April, transplant in May–June, and harvest from September to November. Cooler months support better bulb development.

Yield Per Area: Yields range from 15 to 40 tons per hectare. Early hybrids can exceed 50 tons, with top-performing farms reaching 80+ tons.

Water Requirements: Requires 350–550 mm of water annually. Water 1–3 times per week depending on growth stage and climate.

Soil Requirements: Prefers deep, loamy, well-drained soil with pH 6.0 to 6.8. Avoid compacted or saline soils.

Sunlight Needs: Needs 6–8 hours of direct sunlight daily. Insufficient light reduces bulb size and quality.

Temperature Tolerance: Grows best between 13°C and 24°C. Hardy down to –6°C, but frost can damage young plants.

Fertilizer Needs: Balanced NPK fertilizer supports leaf and bulb development. Micronutrients like sulfur and magnesium may be needed.

Irrigation Needs: Drip irrigation improves water efficiency and reduces disease risk.

Seed Spacing & Density: Rows spaced 20–30 cm apart; plants 8–10 cm apart. Sowing density ranges from 1500 to 2500 seeds per m².
