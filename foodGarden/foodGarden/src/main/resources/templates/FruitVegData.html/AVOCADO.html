AVOCADO

Avocado trees are long-term investments, typically taking 4 to 15 years to bear fruit, though grafted varieties can produce within 2 to 3 years. In South Africa, growth slows during cooler autumn and winter months, while spring and summer bring vigorous new growth and flowering. These trees require well-draining, nutrient-rich soil with a pH between 5 and 7, full sun exposure for 6 to 8 hours daily, and protection from strong winds. Avocados are heavy water consumers, needing 1,000 to 1,300 mm of rainfall annually, especially during fruit set. Ideal temperatures range from 16°C to 29°C—conditions outside this range can hinder growth or damage the tree. With proper care, yields can reach 12 to 16 tons per hectare, with top-performing farms producing up to 22 tons.
