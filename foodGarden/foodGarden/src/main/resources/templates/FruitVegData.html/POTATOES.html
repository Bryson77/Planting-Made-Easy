POTATO

Growth Duration:

Potatoes mature in 90–120 days. Early varieties are ready in 60–80 days, while late-season types take up to 130 days.



Optimal Growing Seasons:

Plant from mid-February to mid-April. Cooler temperatures support tuber formation.



Yield Per Area:

Target 50,000–70,000 plants per hectare. Yields vary based on variety, soil, and irrigation.



Water Requirements:

Requires 500–700 mm of water over the season. Consistent moisture is crucial during tuber bulking.



Soil Requirements:

Prefers well-drained, fertile soil with pH 5.8–6.5. Avoid waterlogged or compacted soils.



Sunlight Needs:

Full sun exposure is essential for healthy foliage and tuber development.



Temperature Tolerance:

Cooler temperatures (15°C–20°C) are ideal. High heat can reduce tuber quality and yield.



Fertilizer Needs:

Requires nitrogen for leaf growth, phosphorus for root development, and potassium for tuber quality.



Irrigation Needs:

Drip or furrow irrigation helps maintain consistent moisture and prevents disease.



Spacing & Density:

Space plants 30 cm apart in rows 75 cm apart. Adjust spacing based on variety and soil type.
