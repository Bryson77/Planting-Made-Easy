ORANGE

Growth Duration: Orange trees typically begin bearing fruit 3 to 5 years after planting, with full production reached around year 8.

Optimal Growing Seasons: Oranges thrive in subtropical climates with mild winters and warm summers. In South Africa, planting is best done in spring or early summer in regions like Limpopo, Mpumalanga, and KwaZulu-Natal.

Yield Per Area: Commercial yields vary by variety and management, ranging from 25 to 70 tons per hectare under optimal conditions.

Water Requirements: Oranges require regular irrigation, especially during flowering and fruit development. Drip or microsprinkler systems are ideal to deliver 500–1,300 mm of water annually depending on climate.

Soil Requirements: Best grown in well-drained sandy loam or loamy soils with a pH between 6.0 and 7.0. Organic matter and proper fertilization improve productivity.

Sunlight Needs: Full sun exposure is essential—at least 6 to 8 hours daily.

Temperature Tolerance: Ideal temperatures range from 13°C to 38°C. Frost and extreme heat can damage fruit and reduce yield.

Fertilizer Needs: Apply balanced NPK fertilizers with micronutrients based on soil analysis. Fertilize three times a year—typically in July, December, and March.

Spacing & Orchard Design: Trees are spaced 3 to 5 meters apart, with rows 4 to 8 meters apart depending on rootstock and variety.
