SWEET POTATO

Growth Duration:

Sweet potatoes take 90 to 150 days to mature. Varieties like Beauregard and Georgia Jet mature faster (90 days), while Jewel and Stokes take up to 120 days.



Optimal Growing Seasons:

Best planted from January to March. Warm soil and long days support vigorous growth.



Yield Per Area:

Yields typically range from 25 to 30 tons per hectare, with top-performing farms exceeding 40 tons.



Water Requirements:

Requires 450 to 600 mm of water during the growing season. While drought-tolerant, consistent moisture improves yield and quality.



Soil Requirements:

Prefers permeable, slightly acidic soil (pH 6.0–6.5) rich in nutrients and organic matter. Avoid compacted or waterlogged soils.



Sunlight Needs:

Needs 6 to 8 hours of full sunlight daily. Shade reduces tuber size and sweetness.



Temperature Tolerance:

Thrives in warm, humid conditions between 29°C and 35°C. Temperatures above 38°C may slow growth but are manageable with irrigation.



Fertilizer Needs:

Requires nitrogen for vine growth, potassium for tuber development, and phosphorus for root health.
