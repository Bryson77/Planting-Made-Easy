APPLE

Fruit-Bearing Timeline:

Standard apple trees take up to 8 years to produce fruit, while dwarf varieties begin yielding in 2–3 years.



Planting Season:

They grow best when planted in spring or winter in mild, moist climates.



Soil Requirements:

Ideal soil should have a pH between 6 and 7 and must be well-drained to prevent waterlogging.



Sunlight & Temperature:

Apple trees need full sunlight and temperatures ranging from 18°C to 24°C for growth, and 21°C to 27°C for fruit development.



Watering Needs:

Watering should be adjusted based on soil type and weather, typically between 18–38 liters every 1-2 weeks.



Fertilization & Irrigation:

Nitrogen-rich compost supports healthy growth, and irrigation systems like soaker hoses or sprinklers are recommended.



Spacing Guidelines:

Proper spacing—ranging from 1.8 to 7.6 meters depending on tree type—ensures optimal sunlight exposure and development.
