LETTUCE

Growth Duration: Lettuce matures in 60 to 90 days depending on the variety. Leaf types grow faster than head-forming varieties like iceberg.

Optimal Growing Seasons: Lettuce thrives in cool weather, ideally between 16°C and 18°C. Plant in early spring or autumn to avoid heat stress and bolting.

Yield Per Area: Yields typically range from 20 to 30 tons per hectare under good management. Proper spacing and pest control help maximize output.

Water Requirements: Requires consistent moisture, especially during germination and head formation. Overwatering can lead to root rot, while underwatering causes bitterness.

Soil Requirements: Prefers well-drained, loamy soil rich in organic matter. Ideal pH is between 5.5 and 7.0. Compost improves structure and fertility.

Sunlight Needs: Full sun is preferred, but partial shade helps in warmer climates to prevent bolting.

Temperature Tolerance: Can grow between 7°C and 18°C, but optimal growth occurs between 16°C and 18°C. High temperatures reduce quality and yield.
