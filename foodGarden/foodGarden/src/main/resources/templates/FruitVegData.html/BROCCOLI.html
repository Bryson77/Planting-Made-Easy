BROCCOLI

Growth Duration: Broccoli takes 70 to 100 days to mature from seed, depending on the variety. Early types are ready in 60–75 days, while late-season varieties may take longer.

Optimal Growing Seasons: Best planted in cooler months—February to April. Ideal temperatures range from 10°C to 20°C, which promote compact head formation.

Yield Per Area: Yields range from 5 to 12 tons per hectare, averaging around 8 tons. Proper spacing, pest control, and soil fertility are key to maximizing output.

Water Requirements: Requires 250–300 mm of water per week. Soil should remain evenly moist, especially during head development.

Soil Requirements: Prefers rich, loamy, well-drained soil with pH between 6.0 and 7.0. Organic matter like compost or aged manure boosts nutrient availability.

Sunlight Needs: Needs 6–8 hours of full sun daily. In hot climates, partial shade can help prevent premature bolting.

Temperature Tolerance: Broccoli thrives in cool conditions, with optimal growth between 16°C and 20°C. Temperatures above 29°C may reduce head quality.

Fertilizer Needs: Requires nitrogen for leafy growth, potassium for plant health, and boron to prevent hollow stems.

Irrigation Needs: Drip irrigation is preferred for consistent moisture and reduced disease risk.

Seed Spacing & Density: Space plants 18–24 inches apart in rows 36 inches apart. Aim for 17,500 to 22,500 plants per acre.
