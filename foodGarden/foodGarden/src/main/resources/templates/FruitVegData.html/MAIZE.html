MAIZE

MAIZE

Growth Duration: Maize matures in 60 to 100 days depending on the variety. Early hybrids grow faster, while late-season types take longer.

Optimal Growing Seasons: In South Africa, plant between late October and mid-December. Timing depends on rainfall onset in dryland regions.

Water Requirements: Requires 500–750 mm of rainfall annually. Supplemental irrigation may be needed during dry spells. Water every 3–7 days depending on growth stage.

Soil Requirements: Prefers deep, well-drained soil with good texture and structure. pH should be above 4.5. Lime may be added to correct acidity.

Sunlight Needs: Full sun is essential for photosynthesis and grain development.

Temperature Tolerance: Flowering occurs best between 19°C and 25°C. Extreme heat or cold can reduce yield.

Fertilizer Needs: Requires phosphorus and potassium during growth. Nitrogen boosts leaf and stalk development.

Irrigation Needs: Drip irrigation improves water efficiency and grain yield. Depth and frequency affect productivity.

Seed Spacing & Density: Space plants 13–15 cm apart. Rows should be spaced to allow airflow and light penetration.
